Welcome to Instrumetriq

See what the crowd on X (Twitter) is saying about any crypto coin - and how derivatives traders are positioned - all while you're on your trading page.

  • Chatter level - is this coin Quiet, Active, Buzzing, or Spiking vs its own 30-day average?
  • Chatter tone - how this coin's sentiment compares to its own 30-day average. Shows whether the mood has shifted.
  • Funding, OI flow, Whale lean - how derivatives traders are positioned right now.
  • Context-aware - opens to the coin you're looking at on Binance, TradingView, or Bybit.

How it works

The chart

Each bar on the chart represents one data cycle (between every 2-4 hours). A taller bar means the coin received more engagement (likes, retweets, reach) during that cycle.

The bar color shows the tone shift: green when sentiment is more positive than usual for this coin (shift above +8), red when more negative than usual (shift below -8), and grey when near the coin's own baseline or when data is insufficient.

Chart timeframes

Use the C / D / W buttons above the chart to switch between three views of the same underlying data:

  • C (Cycle) - one bar per data cycle, covering the last 7 days. This is the most granular view and the default.
  • D (Day) - one bar per calendar day, covering the last 30 days. Each bar is the average engagement for that day. Today's bar updates with each new cycle.
  • W (Week) - one bar per week, covering the last 4 weeks. Each bar is the average engagement across a full 7-day window.

All three views use a rolling window anchored to today, so the chart always shows the most recent data available.

Engagement Coefficient (EC)

For each cycle we calculate an Engagement Coefficient - a single number that captures how much attention a coin is getting on X (Twitter).

EC = (likes + retweets) x log(follower reach) / posts

  • We add up the likes and retweets from all posts about this coin in the cycle.
  • We weigh that by the follower reach of the people posting - a post from a large account counts more than the same post from a tiny one.
  • We divide by the number of posts so a coin with 5 very popular posts scores higher than one with 50 posts that nobody noticed.

In short: EC tells you how much engagement each post is getting, adjusted for the audience size of the people talking.

Why the chart uses a log scale

A coin can fluctuate between EC 0 (nobody talked about it) and EC 20,000+ (a viral spike) across the same chart. If we drew the bars on a plain linear scale, a single spike would make every other bar invisible.

Instead, we compress the bars using a logarithmic scale. Think of it like a volume knob: the difference between silence and a whisper is clearly visible, while the difference between loud and very loud is still shown, but does not wipe out everything else.

  • Zero stays flat - if nobody talked about the coin, the bar is empty.
  • Small values are visible - a quiet cycle still shows as a short bar.
  • Huge spikes are tall but proportional - a viral moment is the tallest bar, but smaller bars remain readable.

Chatter level labels

The label on each card (Quiet, Active, Buzzing, Spiking) compares the latest EC to this coin's own 30-day average. Think of it as "how talkative is this coin right now compared to its usual self?"

  • Quiet - less than half the usual level
  • Active - around normal (0.5x - 2x average)
  • Buzzing - noticeably above normal (2x - 6x average)
  • Spiking - unusual attention (more than 6x average)

Chatter tone

Crypto Twitter is structurally positive - most coins run 60-80% positive on a normal day. Absolute sentiment numbers are meaningless. What matters is whether sentiment has shifted relative to this coin's own baseline.

For each cycle, we compute a raw net score (positive% minus negative%) and compare it to the coin's 30-day rolling average of the same metric. The difference is the shift:

shift = (current net) - (30-day average net)

If a coin normally runs at net +55 and today it's at net +35, the shift is -20 - sentiment has dropped significantly below its usual level, even though the absolute numbers are still positive.

The shift maps to a label:

  • Above +16 - Bullish (much more positive than usual)
  • +8 to +16 - Positive
  • -8 to +8 - Neutral (near baseline)
  • -16 to -8 - Negative
  • Below -16 - Bearish (much more negative than usual)

The color reflects the label: green for Positive and above, red for Negative and below, and grey for Neutral.

This is the same logic used for Chatter Level (EC vs 30-day mean) - relative to the coin's own history, not absolute numbers.

Tone is only shown when we have enough data to be confident (at least 5 posts and high model confidence). Coins with fewer than 30 historical readings show a simplified raw net label while their baseline builds up.

Derivatives data

Funding, OI flow, and Whale lean come from Binance perpetual futures contracts.

  • Funding - shows whether leveraged longs or shorts are paying a premium to hold their positions.
  • OI flow - whether new money is entering (OI rising) or leaving (OI falling) derivatives.
  • Whale lean - which direction the largest traders are positioned, relative to the market.
